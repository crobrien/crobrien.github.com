Journal One:

The article “Best Practices for Form Design” discusses their 20 recommended tips for designers to create forms that are intuitive, legible, and navigable. Their tips included directions such as showing feedback, prioritizing accessibility, and intentional labeling. I found tip number nine “size fields accordingly” to be the most useful because it was not something I had considered before. Sizing fields accordingly means to ensure that the length of a field box reflects the length of the answer that will be typed inside. Overall, employing as many of the recommended practices as possible will not only help users understand what a form is asking for, but it will help ensure they fill it out as efficiently and correctly as possible.
